Description

Phnom Penh is the capital and largest city of Cambodia, located at the confluence of the Mekong, Bassac, and Tonle Sap rivers. It is Cambodia's primary political, economic, and cultural center, known for its vibrant atmosphere and blend of traditional Khmer and French colonial architecture. Once called the "Pearl of Asia," the city features historical landmarks like the Royal Palace and temples, vibrant markets, and the sobering reminders of its past under the Khmer Rouge, such as the Tuol Sleng Genocide Museum and the Killing Fields.

2. When is the best time to visit Phnom Penh? Located in a tropical monsoon climate zone, Phnom Penh has two distinct seasons: the dry season and the rainy season. The dry season lasts from December to May of the following year. Within the dry season, there are two sub-seasons: the cool season (December to February) and the hot season (March to May). The ideal time to visit Phnom Penh is when the weather is dry and cool. At this time, Cambodia often has warm sunshine, perfect for outdoor activities such as beach trips, visiting ancient temples, or exploring tropical forests. Especially during the summer, when many traditional festivals take place, you can immerse yourself in vibrant celebrations such as Chol Chnam Thmay (Khmer New Year), the Bom Chaul Chnam Water Festival, or the Boat Racing Festival. These are wonderful opportunities to explore the unique culture of the Khmer people.
